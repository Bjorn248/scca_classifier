B-Spec Class Questionnaire

B-Spec Class Overview

This questionnaire walks through the B-Spec preparation rules from the SCCA General Competition Rules (GCR). Answer "Yes" or "No" for each section to see whether your car can be built to B-Spec specifications. The overview below explains the intent of the category.

Definition

The B-Spec and C-Spec Categories shall be considered primarily as a form for the membership to race street stock automobiles. Eligibility of cars may be discontinued at any time, for any reason other than competitive stature. The burden of proof lies upon any participant seeking action against another participant. NOTE: A Shop/Service Manual or its equivalent for the specific make, model, and year of automobile is required to be in the possession of each entrant. Manufacturer Shop/Service Manuals may come in the form of printed material, microfiche, CD, DVD, and/or Internet access to manufacturer website databases. It is the responsibility of the competitor to provide the electronic device capable of accessing the data for compliance verification. If Manufacturer Shop/Service Manuals are not available, then the competitor shall have a copy of the official SCCA® Vehicle Technical Sheet (VTS) with them at every event and shall present it for reference when officially requested. The manual is intended to aid SCCA® Technical Scrutineers in identifying parts and the configuration of the automobile. Overhaul procedures that in the slightest way would increase performance are not to be utilized (e.g., milled heads/blocks, porting, etc.). Blueprinting and balancing are inconsistent with the philosophy of this class and are not permitted.

Automobile Eligibility

Only those cars listed on spec lines are eligible to compete. Cars classified will be approved by EPA and DOT for sale in the United States and by Environment Canada for sale in Canada. They shall be models available to the general public for purchase. Additions and deletions of automobiles shall be at the discretion of the SCCA®. The vehicle identification number (VIN) shall correspond with the model automobile classified. At least one VIN plate or stamping shall remain in place on the dashboard or chassis that corresponds with the model automobile classified.

Classification

Classifications of automobiles eligible for competition will be reviewed on an annual basis and will be effective as of January 1st.

These requirements are for Technical & Safety Items.

The following represent the only safety items and modifications permitted and required on automobiles involved in B-Spec and C-Spec competition. Cars must meet comply with the GCR. The addition of safety items not specifically listed is not permitted. No permitted component/modification shall additionally perform a prohibited function.

1. Roll cages shall be contained entirely within the driver/passenger compartment and must comply with GCR Section 9.4, Roll Cages for GT and Production Based Cars.

2. Installation of a fire extinguisher or fire system as specified in GCR Section 9.3, Fire System.

3. Installation of a safety harness system as specified in GCR Section 9.3, Driver's Restraint System.

4. All cars shall run with both front door windows fully open (down) if equipped and shall have a driver's side window safety net per GCR Section 9.3, Window Safety Nets. The OEM window opening on the front doors shall not be filled in with any material, other than the material required to mount a NACA-duct for driver cooling. If used, the NACA-duct shall be mounted in the front, lower, corner of the window opening. The area closed off to mount the NACA-duct shall not exceed 50 squareinches. In rain conditions, a quarter window larger than 50 square-inches may be used in the area normally used to mount the permitted NACA-duct, in an attempt to minimize the amount of water entering the cockpit. Enough open area for the driver to exit in an emergency shall remain open at all times. Rear windows shall be run in the closed (up) position

5. Passive restraint systems shall be deactivated.*

6. Air bag systems shall be disarmed and may be removed.* If so equipped, the rolling door lock mechanism may be deactivated by unplugging the components.

7. The driver's seat (only) shall be replaced with a one-piece, bucket-type race seat. Standard seat tracks/brackets may be modified, reinforced, and/or removed to facilitate replacement mountings provided they perform no other function.

8. Steering lock mechanisms may be removed or disabled.

9. An electrical master switch may be installed. If car is used on public roads, these items should be replaced, reactivated, rearmed, etc. when not in competition. 9.1.10. B-Spec and C-Spec Categories Specifications


Does your car meet the requirements below?
These requirements are for Vehicle Preparation.

The following represents the only items authorized in the preparation of a vehicle for B-Spec and C-Spec competition other than safety items as required in Section 9.1.7.D, Technical and Safety Items. Modifications shall not be made unless specifically authorized herein. No permitted component/modification shall additionally perform a prohibited function.

1. Towing eyes per GCR Section 9.3, Towing Eyes, shall be fitted.

2. Hubcaps, wheel trim rings, jack, and tools shall be removed.

3. All mud flaps shall be removed.

4. Spare wheels and tires may be removed. Spare tire covers and trunk mats and/or trunk carpeting shall be removed if they present a hazard as a loose flying or flapping object.

5. All adjustments shall be at the manufacturer's specification and/or within the manufacturer's specified tolerances unless they are specifically allowed in the GCR (example camber angle).

6. Tires:

a. B-Spec tires shall be 205/50R15 Goodyear Eagle RS (Dry tire) Catalog #D4850 or P205/50ZR15 Goodyear Eagle RS-R (Wet tire) Catalog #D4851. Mixing wet and dry tires is not permitted. Tires may not be modified. All B-Spec competitors are required to place two (2) 12-inch or larger "Goodyear" decals on their cars, one on each side of the car above the front tire. For C-Spec: Tires shall be 225/45R17. Mixing of wet and dry tires is not permitted. Tires must be run unmodified

7. Wheels: All wheels shall be one-piece metal castings or metal two piece welded. All four wheels must be the same dimensional offset. Any offset is permitted so long as the tire tread (that portion of the tire that contacts the ground) may not protrude beyond the fender opening when viewed from the top perpendicular to the ground. Aftermarket wheel studs and or wheel bolts are allowed. Wheel bolts may be replaced with studs and nuts. Wheel spacers are allowed, but must be the same thickness on all four corners. B-Spec Required wheel/rim diameter is fifteen inches (15"). Maximum wheel/rim width is seven inches (7"). Minimum Wheel/rim weight shall be 13 lbs. C-Spec Required wheel/rim diameter is seventeen inches (17"). Maximum wheel/rim width is eight inches (8"). Minimum Wheel/rim weight shall be 17 lbs.

8. Radio/stereo audio equipment and air conditioning refrigerant systems are the only options permitted and may be non-manufacturer, standard equipment. Two-way radios may be used. Removal of rear window wipers and wiper motor is allowed.

9. Fuel, coolant, oil fluid hoses and clamps, oil filters, fuel filters, and belts (fan, alternator, etc.) may be substituted with others of equivalent manufacturer's specifications.

10. Brake fluid: May be substituted with other equivalent manufacturer's specification.

11. Lubricants: Lubricants may be substituted with any lubricant. Additives are unrestricted.

12. Spark Plugs: Authorized spark plugs listed in spark plug manufacturer's application charts, owner's manual, manufacturer's shop/service manual, or equivalent justified by one cross reference chart. Use of resistor- or non resistor spark plug allowed.

13. "Special performance" specifications from the manufacturer that go beyond those listed on a specification line for a car will not be considered valid. In the case of service circulars, recalls, etc., the burden of proof of validity will be upon the competitor.

14. Ride height: For B-Spec Minimum ride height is six (6) inches, for C-Spec Minimum ride height is five and a half (5½) inches, to be measured without driver at the lowest point of the rocker panels, but not to include welded seams or fasteners. A vehicle may have a ride height listed in the spec line, the competitor must conform to the spec line.

15. Batteries: may be replaced with those of alternate manufacture provided they are of lead acid chemistry, weigh at least 6 lbs., and are sized to be able to start the car. Battery must remain in stock location. Additional hold-down brackets are allowed. 9.1.10. B-Spec and C-Spec Categories Specifications

16. Weight: The minimum weight as listed on the B-Spec and C-Spec line is with driver and ballast may be added to meet the listed minimum weight. Refer to section 9.3. Ballast, for placement of the ballast. If a cool suit system is utilized, the cool suit system shall be weighed with the car as it came off the track.

A. The ballast shall be in segments no lighter than ten (10) pounds and no heavier than fifty (50) pounds.

B. Each segment shall be fastened with a minimum of two (2) one-half inch (1/2") bolts and positive lock nuts of SAE Grade 5/Metric 8.8 or better, and shall utilize large diameter, load distributing washers.

17. Fuel: Only the fuel type specified by the owner's or manufacturer's shop/service manual may be used. Refer to GCR Section 9.3, Fuel, for permitted fuel specifications.

18. Removal of Air Conditioning System: The manufacturer's or aftermarket air conditioning system may be removed. Items that serve a dual purpose, such as the alternator/air conditioning compressor bracket, may not be substituted. Idler pulleys and belts may be substituted as needed when compressor is removed.

19. Removal of radio and speaker components is permitted. Removal of horn and cruise control system is permitted.

20. A radiator screen of minimum one-fourth inch (1/4") mesh may be added in front of the radiator and contained within the bodywork.

21. Air filter elements may be substituted with other air filters of equivalent specifications and fit in the standard location with no modifications. The filter element must be substantiated by a minimum of one (1) manufacturer cross-reference for specific vehicle application.

22. Any brake pad or lining may be used. Metal or plastic dust shields may be removed or modified by removing material.

23. Standard replacement brake rotors/drums may be obtained from sources other than the original manufacturer provided they are the exact equivalent.

24. Interior mirror(s) may be replaced with a multi-panel type mirror, but shall not extend beyond the confines of the interior.

25. OEM or approved primary/upstream catalysts are required unless a vehicle's spec line lists an approved header or specifically permits to removing the catalyst. The primary/upstream oxygen sensor must be installed and functional. Secondary oxygen sensors and catalysts may be removed. The exhaust system downstream of the primary catalyst/approved header is free provided the system retains the original routing and exits the body in the approximate location(s) as the OEM system. Single exhaust systems that are cosmetically split into dual outlets may use a single outlet. OEM heat shields and/or exhaust heat wrap may be used.

26. Aftermarket steering wheels, and their required mounting modifications, are permitted. Removable steering wheels are permitted.

27. Data acquisition systems (GPS or accelerometer-based), lap timers, and coaching systems are allowed. Tachometer, shift lights, oil pressure and temperature, coolant pressure and temperature, air-fuel ratio, and cylinder head temperature sensors may be added, integrated into a data acquisition system, and/or displayed to the driver from entry into the grid until the end of impound and stewards' actions connections to the CAN bus are permitted only for the purpose of reading CAN data to monitor and display vehicle and driver performance using data acquisition systems, dashboards and indicators. All connected systems must remain 'silent' on the CAN bus: they may receive data but must not be capable of sending or altering data on the bus, nor may they be capable of changing ECU behavior. Collected data may be displayed to the driver. Devices must be securely mounted. External devices may not be connected to the car during impound without the permission of a steward or tech official.

28. Sunroofs are only permitted if installed by the manufacturer of the vehicle. If installed, they must be run in the closed position and securely bolted in place unless the operating rails adequately secure the panel. Glass panels are permitted. The glass sunroof may be replaced with a metal panel and all its associated mechanical components may be removed. The panel must retain the shape of the glass sunroof.

29. Hatchback "privacy covers" must be completely removed.

30. Cosmetic plastic engine covers may be removed. 9.1.10. B-Spec and C-Spec Categories Specifications

31. Original brake and clutch hoses may be replaced by braided stainless steel brake lines and clutch lines.

32. Interiors may be removed including seats, seat brackets, carpet, carpet padding, rear door panels, OEM seat belts, interior trim, and headliners. Front door window glass, window operating mechanisms, inner door trim panel, armrest, map pockets, wiring harnesses for front door locks, power mirrors, seat wiring, etc., and inside front door latch/lock operating mechanism may be removed. Original radio/stereo audio equipment and air conditioner refrigerant systems may be removed. Heater cores, and hoses, must remain except duct work under seats. Duct work behind the dash may be trimmed but not removed to allow for roll cage installation.

33. Competitors may use the OEM suspension, the approved suspension kit on a vehicle's spec line, or any B14Bilstein shock/strut with no modifications except as required for mounting or to achieve allowed camber. Therequired parts to install a B14 kit must be submitted for approval and added to a vehicle's spec line. B-Spec maximum spring rates are 500 pounds front and rear. C-Spec maximum spring rates are 600 pounds frontand 800 pounds rear. Helper springs made of thin flat wire that maintain spring position under full travel arepermitted. Tender springs are not permitted. Springs may be fastened to the body to keep the spring seatedunder full travel. A maximum of 3.5 degrees negative camber is allowed on front and rear suspensions. Strut suspensions may adjust camber using any camber plate, but it must not alter caster. Caster shall be within factory specifications. Strut suspensions may adjust camber using slotted upper strut plates, eccentric bolts (crash bolts), and/ or slotted strut mounting holes at the spindle. Multilink suspensions may adjust camber using approved parts listed on a vehicle's spec line. If upper strut slotted plates are used, they shall be located on existing chassis structure and may not serve as reinforcement for that structure. Modifications to the top of the strut tower may be made for camber adjustment only. Modifications to the existing holes must only move the strut top inboard even if the existing slots would translate the top of the strut rearward. Front and rear toe settings are free. Rear toe adjustments on beam axle cars may be achieved using shims. Bump stops may be deleted but cannot be modified or substituted and shall serve no other purpose. Adjustable sway bar end links may be used on all cars. Sway bars may be disconnected and removed.

34. ECU/PCM: All electronic control systems (ECU/PCM/ABS, etc) must be unaltered, model-appropriate US domestic market OE devices unless otherwise permitted on the spec line. Alternate engine calibrations are allowed but no changes are permitted and no device capable of altering ECU characteristics may be present in the car from the time the car enters the grid until after impound and any stewards' actions. OBD II compliant data correct for the car must be present at the data link connector. Malfunction codes ('check engine light') may be present. Manufacturers or competitors may submit ABS, stability control and speed limiter override procedures or modules for approval and listing on the spec line.

35. Fenders and wheel openings shall remain unmodified. It is permitted to roll under or flatten any interior lip on the wheel opening for tire clearance. Cars with plastic/composite fenders may remove any interior wheel opening lip, but the resulting material edge shall be no thinner than the basic fender material thickness. Non metallic inner fender liners may be removed.

36. An oil catch can is permitted.

37. Installation of hood pins is recommended but not required. If hood pins are used, the factory release and cable must be removed, but the secondary latch may be retained. Vehicles equipped with plastic release cables may replace them with steel units. The OEM hood release latch may be relocated.

38. Brake ducts are permitted, but they must serve no other purpose. Duct openings may be created by the removal of the fog lights. Alternatively, duct openings may be created by opening 2 sections up to 14.5 square inches each of stock false grills originally located in the front fascia, or radiator shroud, but in this case while fog lamps may be removed. Fog light holes must be completely covered. The stock headlamp location is not permitted for brake ducting. 9.1.10. B-Spec and C-Spec Categories Specifications

39. Restrictor - B-Spec and C-Spec cars whose spec line require a restrictor will follow the definition of a Flat Plate Restrictor (FPR) outlined in Appendix F with only one exception, B-Spec and C-Spec requires the thickness of the Restrictor to be 0.050" to 0.079.

40. All B-Spec and C-Spec cars in post-race impound at all Majors and Super Tour races shall open their hoods and hatches/trunks for the purpose of visual inspection by other competitors. Competitors may thereafter initiate a protest as permitted under the GCR.

41. Positive fasteners may be used to attach the bumper caps to the OEM bumper cap clips and may include a machine screw and T-nut or a rivet-nut. Pointed screws such as self-drilling/tapping or sheet metal screws are prohibited.

42. All cars which use throttle body to intake fasteners directly into a plastic intake, are allowed to convert the intakes to accommodate a metal threaded insert. This is done for the sole reason that repeated removal of the restrictor plate wears the threads in the intake and eventually causes the need to replace the intake.

43. Aftermarket shift knobs are allowed and shift levers may be bent or extended, however the shift linkage/cables must remain stock and may not be modified. Approved aftermarket shift linkage bushings are permitted. Bore x
